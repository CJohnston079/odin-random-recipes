Better Banana Bread

Whole wheat flour, brown sugar, honey, and cardamom provide rich, earthy accents to the ripe banana flour, providing new depths of flavor to an otherwise bland quick bread.

  • Prep: 20 minutes
  • Cook: 40 minutes
  • Total: 1 hour
  • Yield: 2 loaves

    3 large, very ripe bananas

  1. Mash bananas in a large oven-safe bowl.
  2. Place bowl in cold oven and preheat oven to 350°F. Remove from preheating oven once bananas have warmed slightly and they have released some of their juices, about 5 to 10 minutes. Set aside.

    3. 2 eggs, lightly beaten

    96g / ½ cup brown sugar

    ⅓ cup extra-virgin olive oil or vegetable oil

    84g / ¼ cup honey or agave syrup

    1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  3. In a medium-sized bowl, beat eggs, sugar, oil, honey, and vanilla. Mix until well combined.

    4. Reserved bananas

  4. Add bananas and mix well to combine. Set aside.

    5. 124g / 1 cup all-purpose flour

    120g / 1 cup white whole wheat flour

    2 ½ teaspoon ground cardamom

    1 teaspoon baking powder

    1 teaspoon baking soda

    1 teaspoon kosher salt

    1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

  5. In a separate, medium bowl whisk flours, salt, baking soda, baking powder, and spices.

    6. Reserved banana mixture

  6. Add flour mixture to reserved banana mixture and stir to combine quickly.

    7. ⅓ cup plain yogurt, buttermilk, or sour cream

    1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

  7. Add yogurt (or buttermilk or sour cream) and walnuts (if desired), and stir to combine.
